### Part #1 Some Principles Of Prophecy

**Scripture Song:** Let the word of Christ – Colossians 3:16

Welcome to Hope Sabbath School—an in-depth interactive study of the Word of God. We are beginning a new series of studies on Bible Prophecies and Stories for the Last Days. Today’s topic: **Some Principles Of Prophecy.**

1. God’s challenge in revealing truth
	- a. What challenge does God face when seeking to reveal truth to the human family? Isaiah 55:8-9
	- b. How does the testimony of the Psalmist David reinforce this challenge? Psalm 139:1-6 (see also Psalm 147:5)
	- c. What was the Apostle Paul’s testimony about the challenge of understanding God’s ways? Romans 11:33, 1 Corinthians 13:12

2. God’s revelation through the prophets
	- a. Hebrews 1:1-2, Amos 3:7 What is one important way God reveals truth to the human family?
	- b. How can we be certain God wants us to understand His revelation through the prophets? Revelation 1:1- 3, Matthew 24:15, etc.
	- c. What is the role of the Holy Spirit in this process of revelation? 2 Peter 1:20-21, John 16:13

3. How to study God’s revelation in the Bible
	- a. What counsel did the Apostle Paul give to Timothy about studying the Word of God? 2 Timothy 3:14-17
	- b. How did Jesus help His disciples understand God’s revelation in the Scriptures? Luke 24:27,44-45
	- c. What important principle do these texts reveal about how to study God’s revelation in the Scriptures? Isaiah 28:9-10
	- d. How can we experience the guidance of the Holy Spirit when studying God’s revelation in the Bible? Luke 11:13
	- e. Why is the counsel of James so important as we study God’s revelation in the Bible? James 1:5
	- f. Share a time when you experienced joy as the Holy Spirit helped you to understand God’s revelation in the Bible (particularly the prophecies of Daniel and Revelation).

4. The use of symbols in God’s revelation
	- a. What are some symbols used in Bible prophecy? (Daniel 7:2-3, Revelation 1:16, 12:1, etc.)
	- b. What can we learn from Daniel’s account in Daniel 7:7-8,24-25?
	- c. Why did God frequently use symbols when revealing truth to the prophets? (see Luke 8:10)

5. Jesus, the Center of God’s revelation
	- a. John 5:39-40 What important truth did Jesus reveal about God’s revelation through the prophets?
	- b. Why is it vitally important to remember that Jesus is the Center of God’s revelation? Hebrews 1:1-3
	- c. When did you come to realize that Jesus is the Center of God’s revelation to you personally?
