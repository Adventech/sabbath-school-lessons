### Someone is Listening

In the middle of the two contrasting responses, a follower of Jesus named Mary did something that, at first glance, seems out of place. Lazarus’ friends gathered for a feast to celebrate his resurrection. The feast was held in the house of Simon, a leper who had been healed (Mark 14:3). Everyone was thankful for Jesus’ miracles on behalf of Simon and Lazarus. Mary was thankful too, not only for what happened to them but also for how Jesus saved her from a life of darkness and oppression. She slipped up behind Jesus and began to anoint His feet with a flask of very expensive and aromatic oil of spikenard. She wiped His feet with her hair, and Luke’s account mentions that she washed His feet with her tears (Luke 7:38, 44). It was clearly a heartfelt moment of overwhelming gratitude.

The sweet aroma drew everyone’s attention, but when they saw what was happening, they had no sense of appreciation for her gift to Jesus. Instead, there was scorn. In Luke’s account, Simon looked down on her because “she is a sinner” (Luke 7:39). Here in John’s Gospel, Judas Iscariot expressed scorn as well (John 12:4–6). Judas claimed she was wasting the expensive gift on Jesus and argued that it should have been sold and the proceeds given to the poor. John clarified that Judas’ outrage wasn’t rooted in righteousness but in greed, as Judas had been stealing from the group’s money box.

At this point, Mary likely felt ashamed and embarrassed. Thankfully, Jesus stepped in and affirmed her. He declared that Mary had not just done a good thing but seemed to be the only one actually listening to what He had been saying all along—because she was preparing His body for His death and burial (John 12:7).

Consider what her gift must have meant to Jesus at that stage. No one else appeared to understand what He had been saying or how difficult the coming events would be for Him. Mary didn’t give Him a simple thank-you card; she poured out a potent, fragrant offering. Perhaps this offering became a reminder to Him during His closing hours that at least one person would respond to the crucifixion and it would all be worth it. As He was harassed, abused, and tempted to believe that no one cared, that fragrance would remind Him of the truth. At least there would be one, and for Him, that would be enough.
