### Enduring Hardship

Knowing what the disciples would endure in the coming days and years, Jesus spent a significant amount of time encouraging them in John 13–17. He assured them that the Holy Spirit would be their Helper and prepared them for the reality that hardships would come. Human encouragement often attempts to soften reality because we struggle to be honest about what lies ahead. However, Jesus struck a balance between being both encouraging and straightforward about the coming challenges.

In John 15, Jesus explained to the disciples that the world would hate them—primarily because it hated Him first. Since the disciples were no longer of the world, the world would reject them. But embedded in the warning is a rich promise: “You are not of the world, but I chose you out of the world, therefore the world hates you” (John 15:19). God has chosen us! This brings balance to the difficult news. He was not saying, “You will endure hardship because I don’t care about you.” Rather, He was saying, “You will endure hardship because the world hates Me, and I have chosen you.” This completely shifts our perspective. How often have we encountered hardship and wondered why God didn’t prevent it? Or assumed that He was acting against us? According to Jesus, we face hardship because He has chosen us, not because He has abandoned us.

Jesus continued by revealing that the world’s hatred is not directed just at Him; it’s ultimately directed at the Father who sent Him. This truth is difficult. Many of the people who hated the disciples claimed to be followers of God the Father, yet their hatred for Jesus exposed their true hearts: they hated the Father as well. Jesus came to reveal His Father’s character, which stirred up fierce opposition from the religious leaders. Jesus warned His disciples that they would face the same level of hostility.

Jesus made it clear that Satan is the ruler of this world and that things will not be easy while we’re here. The world will not be for us. The good news is that because God is for us, He will help us through the challenges and persecution that Jesus Himself faced. Jesus could endure the cruel hatred of the world because he was secure in His Father’s love. When we are hated by the world, God’s love can hold us too.
