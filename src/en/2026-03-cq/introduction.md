### The Closing Scenes

The Bible makes an incredible claim about God in 1 John 4:8: God is love. It doesn’t say He merely possesses a certain amount of love or is occasionally loving, but that He is the embodiment and source of all true love. In 1 Corinthians 13:5, we learn that love, by its very nature, is not self-centered—it focuses on others. Nowhere is this “other-centered” love more vividly displayed than in the closing scenes of Jesus’ life on earth.

The final moments of Christ’s life were filled with a rollercoaster of emotions. Those moments bore witness to the story of all stories and contain the key to experiencing salvation and developing a true and intimate relationship with our Creator. It’s also a narrative that gives profound insight into Jesus’ mindset—and into where He placed His focus—during His most difficult moments.

You may not at first believe it, but His focus was on you.

While enduring the most horrific death imaginable, facing rejection from His nation and religious leaders, experiencing His disciples abandon Him in His greatest hour of need, and even being utterly heartbroken by the apparent absence of His Father’s comforting presence, Jesus still, miraculously, chose to focus on us—on you, on me, on all of humanity. It’s truly mind-boggling.

In this quarter of inVerse, we will explore the closing scenes of Jesus’ life to understand what it took to realize humanity’s complete reconciliation and restoration. The first six lessons focus on Jesus’ final actions and teachings before His arrest. In John 13–17, we find a unique section of Jesus’ teachings not recorded anywhere else in the Gospels. These chapters are filled with powerful promises and lessons designed to carry the disciples through their darkest hours—and I believe they’re also meant to guide us through the closing scenes of earth’s history. We’ll take our time, studying these chapters one at a time to fully grasp their depth. The remaining seven lessons focus on Jesus’ journey from Gethsemane to the resurrection. It’s a heavy but hopeful study—one I believe has the power to transform our lives if we approach it with open hearts.

Some may feel they already know this story. After all, all Christians know Jesus died for us. What more could we possibly cover in thirteen weeks? I once felt the same way. God’s love had already radically changed my life; I had converted and eventually entered full-time ministry. However, ten years into my Christian walk and four years into ministry, something happened that yet again changed everything.

On a warm summer day in July 2014, I heard a preacher share the simple story of the cross in a way I had never encountered it before. It wrecked me—and everyone else who heard it. I doubt there was a dry eye in the room. That day, I made a decision that transformed how I viewed ministry: I vowed never to commit the sin of neglecting to share this story ever again.

In the years since that day, I’ve seen this story change countless lives. Every time I’ve shared it, it’s brought me back to the foot of the cross, just as I first encountered it. Amazingly, I still cry every time I tell it.

I believe this journey will be worth your time. It might even change your life, just as it has changed mine and countless others’ over the centuries.
