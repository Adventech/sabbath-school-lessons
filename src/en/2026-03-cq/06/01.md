**Read This Week’s Passage:** John 17

### Praying for Bigger Things

Several years ago, I joined some friends in praying for their family. We set aside time over a few weeks to lift up the challenges they were facing, and God did something truly special. We witnessed revival in their children’s lives, reconciliation between long estranged families in their church, and many other incredible things. However, what stood out to me more than anything was _how_ this person prayed. It was different from anything I had heard before. They prayed as if they knew they were in God’s presence. They slowed down and spoke to Him like a friend. That experience gave me a glimpse of the power of intercessory prayer.

I saw God move powerfully in people’s lives in response to those prayers. The instance that stands out the most is when I was praying with this friend for one of their children. We carried a burden for them to have a spiritual breakthrough. As we prayed, I felt a deep conviction that God was calling me to pray bigger. The boldest prayer I could think of was asking God to give this person a life-altering encounter with Him. My friend felt convicted to join me in this prayer. So I prayed again, even more boldly, asking God to make it happen that coming Friday—on Halloween, a day often associated with darkness. I asked Him to bring light into their lives on that very day. That prayer was on Wednesday.

We continued praying each day, and on Friday night, I received a text from my friend saying that God had heard us. They didn’t have all the details yet, but they knew He had answered. The next morning, I awoke to a fresh blanket of snow covering the ground. I felt God nudging me, whispering that He had made the situation as white as snow. This was the day after Halloween, in Tennessee, of all places—a rare sight. Later, I learned what had happened. That night, my friend’s child was singing at an evangelistic series when God spoke to his heart. He convicted him that the life he wanted and the life God desired for him was completely different. That conviction led to a powerful transformation in his life. My friend and I knew, without a doubt, that God had heard our prayers. That experience taught me the incredible power of intercessory prayer and to not be afraid to pray for _bigger_ things that glorify Him.

I don’t know about you, but I’ve always wished I could go back in time and witness Jesus’ words and actions firsthand. The four Gospels speak of many events from His life—moments in which He touched people’s hearts and completely changed their lives. However, there is so much we don’t know. John even said at the conclusion of his Gospel: “And there are also many other things that Jesus did, which if they were written one by one, I suppose that even the world itself could not contain the books that would be written” (John 21:25).

John was the last of the four Gospel writers—and only the second eyewitness account among them—and he included details we never would have known otherwise. He took us back to Creation itself, establishing Jesus’ pre-existent nature with amazing clarity. He recorded Jesus’ gripping conversation with Nicodemus, the story of the woman at the well, the healing of the man at the pool of Bethesda, the blind man’s healing in John 9, the raising of Lazarus in John 11, and, as we have already discussed, the powerful teachings of John 13–16. The list goes on.

Jesus’ prayer in John 17 is among the most powerful passages John wrote. This prayer provides a window into Jesus’ heart and shows the close connection He had with His Father.
