As we journey through this study, it’s important to engage deeply with the material and reflect on its implications for our faith and daily lives. In your Sabbath School class (or Bible study group), use the outline of questions below to lead a thoughtful discussion based on this week’s main passage and supporting verses.

**Pilate and Herod agree:**

1. What accusations were brought to Pilate, and what was his response? Luke 23:1–5
2. Why didn’t Jesus show Herod a miracle like he wanted? Luke 23:6–12
3. What did Pilate conclude about Jesus, and what did he wish? Luke 23:13–22

`Personal reflection: Pilate and Herod became friends after Jesus’ trial, and the Pharisees and Sadducees worked together to crucify Jesus. What’s the difference between the right kind of unity and the wrong kind of unity?`

**Pilate concedes:**

1. What concession did Pilate make to try to appease the crowd? Luke 23:16, 22
2. How did Barabbas compare to Jesus? What did the crowd’s choice say about their view of Jesus and their state of mind at that point? Luke 23:18, 19
3. What accusation finally pushed Pilate to yield to the crowd’s demands? John 19:12

`Personal reflection: Consider how you would respond to someone who asks, “What is truth?” (John 8:38).`

**Carrying the cross:**

1. How was Jesus treated before the soldiers made Him carry His cross? Mark 15:16–20
2. Who picked up the cross when Jesus was unable to carry the load? Mark 15:21; John 19:17

`Personal reflection: What does Jesus’ physical inability to carry the cross teach us about vulnerability and relying on support from others? Why is it so hard for us to seek help with the burdens we bear?`

**Key takeaways to remember:**

- Pilate knew the right thing to do, but his fear of losing control and public favor led him to surrender truth itself, showing us just how dangerous it is to ignore conviction for the sake of convenience.
- The crowd’s choice to release Barabbas over Jesus reminds us that whenever we cling to sin instead of surrendering to Christ, we’re making the very same exchange—and yet, Jesus still stands in our place, offering mercy we don’t deserve.
- Jesus collapsing under the weight of His cross reminds us that needing help doesn’t make us weak; it makes us human. Even our Savior needed help. We’re not meant to carry our burdens alone.
