As we journey through this study, it’s important to engage deeply with the material and reflect on its implications for our faith and daily lives. In your Sabbath School class (or Bible study group), use the outline of questions below to lead a thoughtful discussion based on this week’s main passage and supporting verses.

**The advantage of the Spirit:**

1. How fierce did Jesus say the persecution against the disciples would be? John 16:1–4
2. Despite the persecution and Jesus’ devastating departure, why did the disciples have more advantages after Jesus left than before? John 16:5–11
3. What are some of the Holy Spirit’s important roles identified in John 16:8–14?
4. How can we better cooperate with the Holy Spirit and receive more of Him?

`Personal reflection: Compare the advantages we have today to the advantages the disciples had when they walked with Jesus. In what ways do modern believers have more?`

**Pain and sorrow:**

1. What assurance did Jesus give His followers for when we suffer terrible pain and sorrow? John 16:20–22
2. What does God do with our sadness? Ps. 30:11; Isa. 61:1–3

`Personal reflection: What’s an example in your own life of Jesus turning a sorrowful situation into one of joy?`

**Loved through it all:**

1. How deeply do you think the disciples cherished the promise of God’s love after they scattered and were trying to recover from their failures? John 16:27, 32
2. Where can we find true peace in a world of such chaos and tribulation? How do we take hold of such peace? John 16:33

`Personal reflection: Identify someone in your life who needs to hear that “the Father Himself loves you.” How can you share that with them this week?`

**Key takeaways to remember:**

- With the Holy Spirit, we have more advantages now than the disciples in Jesus’ physical presence had.
- Pain is not without purpose. Jesus uses our suffering and sorrow to create something beautiful, leading to a joy no one can take away.
- Jesus knew the disciples would abandon Him, but He still assured them that “the Father Himself loves you.” He assures us of the same.
