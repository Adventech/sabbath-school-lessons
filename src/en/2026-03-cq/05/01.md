**Read This Week’s Passage:** John 16

### A Difficult Season

The promises Jesus made to His disciples in John 16 have brought me repeated waves of comfort in different seasons of life. The first was right after I graduated from Bible college. I felt a distinct impression that He was promising to provide me with a life companion. Over the next ten years, I encountered multiple people whom I believed were the fulfillment of that promise. I experienced moments of deep faith and trust followed by feelings of neglect and abandonment. It was a difficult season, to say the least.

At the beginning of that journey, I was a brand new Adventist. I didn’t fully understand how God would fulfill His promise, and in my struggle to figure out what He was doing—and who I truly was—I caused a lot of problems.

About five years after receiving that promise, I began to recognize my own brokenness and realize I needed to address it. Over time, I became aware of much unresolved trauma in my life, resulting in unhealthy attachment styles. These issues were at the root of many of my struggles. Through therapy, deep study of the Word, and reflection on my own story, I found healing and a growing contentment in being loved by God—regardless of whether I ever received the companion He seemed to have promised.

One passage God continually brought to me throughout that difficult journey was John 16:20–24. Whenever things became tough, He reminded me that while the pain was real, a time would come when I would receive the promise, and the joy of its fulfillment would overshadow the sorrow of waiting.

Ten years after the initial promise, I met the beautiful woman who eventually became my wife. Looking back, I can see how the disappointments and heartbreaks I endured led me to do some serious soul-searching and to pursue healing. God had taken my wife on a similar journey. She, too, had encountered heartbreaks and wounds, but she leaned into the healing process. When we met, we both sensed that the other understood what a healthy relationship needed to look like. More importantly, we were both willing to put in the work and use the tools we had gained to navigate the challenges of marriage.

God has truly been faithful. Those tools have helped us tremendously, and I couldn’t have found a more wholesome, beautiful person with whom to walk through life. I can hardly remember the hard times I went through before I reached this stage. I know it was difficult, but the wounds and excruciating struggles feel like distant memories. The scars aren’t even there; only the joy of walking in the fulfillment of God’s promises remains.

As I write this account, my wife is twenty weeks pregnant. Her pregnancy has come with multiple challenges that have stretched and tested our faith. Thank God, our baby is healthy, but the toll on my wife’s health has been far greater than we anticipated. Hyperemesis, severe light sensitivity and motion sickness, acid reflux, and other complications have made it hard for her to fully enjoy the process of carrying our little girl.

I know many people have faced even greater difficulties during pregnancy, and some have struggled to conceive at all. We are counting our blessings amid these challenges. What’s given me great consolation is knowing that one day, no matter the suffering it took to bring our precious little one into the world, the moment we get to hold her and look into her little eyes, all the hardships will fade into the background.

Jesus sees the challenges ahead, and He promises that what’s on the other side will be worth the cost.
