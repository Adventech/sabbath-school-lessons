### Read for This Week’s Lesson
Daniel 2:20–23; Daniel 6:10; Genesis 5:22–24; Exodus 33:15–23; Exodus 32:31, 32.

> <p>Memory Verse</p>
> “I love the Lord, because he heard me when I called [prayed] to him. He listened [paid attention] carefully to me. When I need help, I will always pray to him, for as long as I live” (Psalm 116:1, 2, EASY).

What do you think would happen if you hardly talked to your best friend or to your husband or wife? Very soon your relationship would be in trouble, right? In the same way, prayer is an important part of a strong relationship with God. Prayer is an important habit. If we don’t pray often and always, we will wander away from the Lord sooner or later.

In the Bible, we learn about the lives of people who prayed. We can learn how their prayers changed their relationship with God for the better. Their stories teach us how to pray and what we may pray for. We can learn from their stories how prayers changed the lives of other people. For sure, our prayer life changes not only ourselves but other people’s lives, too.

The topic of prayer is big. There is more to learn than we can study in the next two weeks. This week, we will learn some important lessons from people in the Bible who prayed. Their prayers will show us that prayer helps us to build a strong relationship with God. What else can we learn from the prayer lives of these people in the Bible?
