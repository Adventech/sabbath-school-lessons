### Unity in Christ

Welcome to Hope Sabbath School—an in-depth interactive study of the Word of God. We are continuing a series on Letters to New Christians. Today’s topic: UNITY IN CHRIST.

**Scripture Song:** It is good to give thanks – Psalm 92:1-4

- The problem of cliques in the church
	- 1 Corinthians 1:10 What appeal does Paul make at the beginning of his letter to Christians in Corinth? 
	- Why did Paul make this appeal for unity and harmony among the believers? 1 Corinthians 1:12-13
	- Why is it so easy for cliques to form in churches, resulting in divisions and contentions?
	- Why are divisions and contentions so debilitating and damaging as Christians seek to share the good news about Jesus with others? John 13:35, John 17:21-23
	- What is the difference between a clique and a healthy small group?
- Perfectly joined together
	- 1 Corinthians 1:10 How is it possible for imperfect human beings to be perfectly joined together? 1 Corinthians 1:4-9, Colossians 3:12-14, Romans 5:5
	- What does it mean to be of “the same mind”?  Philippians 2:1-8
	- How is it possible to have a diversity of opinions and still be of the same mind? 
- Growing in spiritual maturity
	- 1 Corinthians 3:1-2 How does Paul describe the spiritual maturity of Christians in Corinth?
	- How was their spiritual immaturity manifested? 1 Corinthians 3:3-4
	- What is an indication that Christians are growing in their spiritual maturity? 1 Corinthians 2:12-16
	- Who has helped you mature spiritually?
- Leading as servants of Christ
	- 1 Corinthians 4:1-2
	- What teaching of Jesus reminds us of the need to follow His example of servant leadership? Mark 10:42-45
	- What does it mean to be “stewards of the mysteries of Christ”?
	- Is suffering an inevitable experience for servants of Christ? 1 Corinthians 4:9-13, John 15:18-21, Matthew 5:11-12
	- What are your thoughts about Paul’s invitation in 1 Corinthians 4:16? (see also 1 Corinthians 11:1)
	- We all have a circle of influence. Who is looking to you as an example?
	- Why is it always better to keep our eyes focused on Jesus rather than focusing on human leaders, however gifted or charismatic they might be?
	- How can we be assured that we will be faithful examples and faithful stewards in our circle of influence?
