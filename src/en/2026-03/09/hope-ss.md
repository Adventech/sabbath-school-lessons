### Love-driven Ministry

Welcome to Hope Sabbath School—an in-depth interactive study of the Word of God. We are continuing a series on Letters to New Christians. Today’s topic: LOVE-DRIVEN MINISTRY.

**Scripture Song:** It is good to give thanks – Psalm 92:1-4

- Finding comfort in God and sharing comfort
	- 2 Corinthians 1:1-7 What word do you hear repeated at the beginning of Paul’s second letter to Christians in Corinth?
	- Why was a message of comfort encouraging for Christians in Corinth?
	- What was Paul’s testimony about comfort he received from God? 2 Corinthians 1:8-11
	- Share a time when you were comforted by the God of all comfort.
	- Think of a story in the ministry of Jesus where He comforted someone in a time of need.
	- Share a time you were able to comfort someone in need.
- Serving others with simplicity and godly sincerity
	- 2 Corinthians 1:12-14 Why did Paul feel the need to defend his ministry to believers in Corinth? (see also 2 Corinthians 2:17, 4:2)
	- Share an example of someone in the Bible who served others with simplicity and godly sincerity. (Dorcas, Barnabas, etc.)
	- How can we ensure our motives are pure when we are serving others? Psalm 139:1-3,23-24
	- What is an appropriate response if you are accused of improper motives in your service for others?
- Speaking the truth in love
	- What was Paul’s attitude and intention when writing to believers in Corinth? 2 Corinthians 2:1-4
	- How did Paul’s correspondence impact the Christians in Corinth? 2 Corinthians 7:8-12
	- Share another story in the Bible where a prophet had to deliver a message of reproof or correction. (Nathan, Elijah, Paul, etc.)
	- Why is it important to always speak the truth in love, especially when bringing a message of reproof or correction? 2 Corinthians 2:5-8, Ephesians 4:15, Colossians 4:6
	- Share an example from the ministry of Jesus where He brought a message of reproof or correction but spoke the truth in love. (John 8:10-11, Luke 10:41-42, etc.)
	- How can you be certain if God wants you to bring a message of reproof or correction? When is it time to speak and when is it time to remain silent and simply pray? (James 1:5, Psalm 32:8, etc.)
	- Share a time when someone was directed by God to bring a message of reproof or correction to you. How did you respond?
