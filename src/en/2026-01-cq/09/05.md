### inVite

It’s fascinating how something as simple as folded laundry played a crucial role in convincing John of Jesus’ resurrection. John 20:7, 8 says: “And the handkerchief that had been around His head, not lying with the linen cloths, but folded together in a place by itself. Then the other disciple, who came to the tomb first, went in also; and he saw and believed.”

For John, the empty tomb and the neatly folded grave clothes provided more than just a clue; they were the quiet yet powerful evidence that something extraordinary had occurred. First, robbers generally don’t leave folded clothes after a heist. Second, those who knew Jesus knew His mannerisms, and this simple act of order amidst the chaos of the crucifixion confirmed in John’s heart that Jesus had, indeed, risen.

God often uses the simplest things to display His greatest glory. The disciples’ introduction to the resurrection came not with thunder or lightning, but with folded linens and an empty tomb—unmistakable signs of victory. The folded clothes showed that while Jesus’ body had changed, His character had not. He was still the same Jesus. This same compassionate Jesus would intercede on their behalf in heaven. Third, the change of sacred garments was a symbol that the Lamb had become a Priest.

But God didn’t stop with folded clothes. He confirmed the resurrection through various witnesses and signs:

**A Forgiven Woman:** Mary Magdalene, once burdened by her past, was the first to encounter the risen Christ (John 20:14–16). This shows how God’s grace extends to all, no matter their past. Other women also testified of the resurrection.

**The Testimony of Two Disciples:** Peter and John, who both ran to the tomb, saw the folded linen and the absence of Jesus’ body, confirming the miracle (John 20:3–8).

**An Empty Tomb:** The tomb was a silent yet powerful witness. Jesus’ body was not stolen—He had risen, just as He had promised (Luke 24:6).

**The Conviction of the Spirit:** Ultimately, the disciples came to understand the resurrection through the Holy Spirit. Although Jesus had told them He would rise, it was the Spirit that convicted their hearts and opened their eyes to the full truth.

His resurrection was not just a mystical event—it was real, tangible, and confirmed by ordinary details like folded linens and the witness of His followers. The resurrection is the foundation of our faith, the ultimate assurance that because Jesus lives, we too will live. Romans 6:5 reminds us: “For if we have been united together in the likeness of His death, certainly also we shall be united with Him in a resurrection like His.” His resurrection means our resurrection one day too.

**Questions to consider:**

`What does the story of the folded cloth teach us about Jesus?`

`When have you seen God use the simplest things to show His glory and power?`
