### Complete In Christ

Welcome to Hope Sabbath School—an in-depth interactive study of the Word of God. We are continuing our series on Inspired Letters from Prison. Today’s topic from Colossians: COMPLETE IN CHRIST

**Scripture Song:** Rejoice always – 1 Thessalonians 5:16-18, Philippians 4:6

- **An inspired message for all believers**
	- Colossians 2:1 Who were the intended recipients of the Apostle Paul’s inspired letter from prison to Christians in Colossae?
	- What positive outcome did the Apostle Paul desire for all who would read this letter? Colossians 2:2
	- Colossians 2:3 Why did the Apostle Paul emphasize that all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge are found in our heavenly Father and Christ? (see also Colossians 2:9-10)
	- What words of affirmation did the Apostle Paul share with the recipients of this letter? Colossians 2:5
	- How can we remain steadfast in our faith in Christ?
	- What positive admonition did the Apostle Paul give to the recipients of this letter? Colossians 2:6-7
	- What does it mean to walk in Christ Jesus in the same way we received Him as our Savior and Lord?
	- What is your testimony of receiving Jesus as your person Savior and learning to walk with Him?
- **A warning for all believers**
	- Colossians 1:1a Why was the Apostle Paul experiencing “a great conflict” as he thought about the recipients of this letter? (see Colossians 1:4,8)
	- Who was trying to deceive them with persuasive words? Revelation 12:9, Genesis 3:4-5
	- Why is it important to remember that all deceptions have their origin in the Father of Lies? (John 8:44)
	- What specific deceptions were false teachers bringing to followers of Jesus in Colossae and the surrounding region? Colossians 2:11-23
	- What was the handwriting of ordinances that was nailed to the cross? (Colossians 12:14)
	- Why are followers of Jesus no longer obligated to follow the ceremonial laws given to the children of Israel?
	- How do the ceremonial sabbaths differ from the weekly Sabbath given in Eden and reaffirmed in the fourth commandment? Genesis 2:1-3, Exodus 20:8-11
	- What rebuke did Jesus give to false teachers who sought to enforce the traditions of men above the commandments of God? Matthew 15:7-9
	- How can we avoid being led astray by false teachers? Psalm 119:105, Isaiah 8:20, John 7:16-18, 16:13, etc.
