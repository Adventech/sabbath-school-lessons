### Reconciliation And Hope

Welcome to Hope Sabbath School—an in-depth interactive study of the Word of God. We are continuing our series on Inspired Letters from Prison. Today’s topic from Colossians: RECONCILIATION AND HOPE.

**Scripture Song:** Rejoice always – 1 Thessalonians 5:16-18, Philippians 4:6

- **Reconciled to God**
	- Colossians 1:19-20 What does it mean to be reconciled to God?
	- What was our condition before we were reconciled to God? Colossians 1:21
	- What is the result in our lives when we are reconciled to God through Jesus Christ? Colossians 1:22
	- What part are we called to play in this process of reconciliation? Colossians 1:23, 2 Peter 3:18
	- How is this growth in grace possible? Galatians 2:20, Romans 8:1-11, 2 Corinthians 5:17-20, etc.
	- What did your life look like before you were reconciled to God? How did God rescue you?
- **Experiencing suffering as a follower of Christ**
	- Colossians 1:24-25 What suffering was the Apostle Paul experiencing while writing this inspired letter to Christians in Colossae? (Colossians 4:3, Acts 28:16)
	- How did God work good during this time of suffering for the Apostle Paul? Acts 28:30-31, 2 Timothy 2:8-10, Romans 8:28, etc.
	- Share a time you suffered as a follower of Jesus. How did God work good through this time of suffering?
- **The mystery of God revealed**
	- Colossians 1:26 What is the mystery the Apostle Paul is referring to here? (see v.27, 2 Corinthians 5:21)
	- How does the Apostle Paul’s letter to Christians in Ephesus help us to understand this mystery? Ephesians 1:7-10, 3:1-7
	- When did you first come to understand this mystery of God?
	- How have you grown in your awareness of the precious gift of Christ in you, the hope of glory?
- **The power of the Gospel**
	- Colossians 1:28 What was the Apostle Paul’s goal in sharing the mystery of God with others?
	- What does it mean to be “perfect in Christ”?
	- Colossians 1:29 How did the Apostle Paul recognize this sacred work would be accomplished?
	- Share an example from the New Testament of God working powerfully through someone’s witness for Christ.
	- Share a time you saw God working powerfully through you to share the message of His immeasurable, unfailing love.
