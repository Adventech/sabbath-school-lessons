#### inQuire

Share insights from your study this week. Include any questions you might have for the group, as well as discoveries, observations, and new understandings. Below are some questions for discussion with the rest of your study group.

`How many connections can you make between John 12:1–3 and 11:1–3? What differences do you find?`

`Take a moment to enter a prayerful state of mind. Meditate on John 12:1–8. If you had been at the dinner, how would you have reacted? Share your insights.`

`How does the story of Mary washing Jesus’ feet add meaning and significance to the foot-washing service that Seventh-day Adventists practice?`

`How were the disciples reminded that Jesus’ influence and mission extended far beyond the boundaries of Israel? (John 12:20–22.)`

`In what sense must we each die before we can bear fruit? (John 12:24–26.)`

`What kind of emotions do you think Jesus was feeling as He realized His death was near? (John 12:27.)`

`How would Jesus being lifted up on the cross draw all to Him? (John 12:34.)`

`How was the cross a harbinger of judgment? (John 12:30–32, 38.)`

`Consider John’s use of Isaiah to explain the rejection of Christ (John 12:38–41; Isaiah 6:10, 11; 53:1). What insights do you glean?`

`What does it mean to walk in the light (John 12:35, 36)? If you have lost the light, how can you find your way back to it?`
