---
title:  Umtshakazi
date:   23/06/2026
---

Lo gama wayebhaciselwe kwisiqithi sePatmos, umfundi uYohane waanikwa umbono wento eyakwenzeka xa sihlanganiswa kwakhona noThixo ngonaphakade.

``Funda kwiSityhilelo 21:9-11. Faniso luni olwanikezelwayo, kwaye ucinga ukuba kwakutheni ukuze lusetyenziswe?``

Umtshakazi mhle, kwaye ngomhla wakhe womsitho, wonke ubani unqwenela ukumbona. Imini yomtshatho kukujika kwezinto kubomi obutsha nikunye kumtshakazi nomyeni, kwaye kanjalo oku kuyakuba yinyaniso ngobudlelane bethu noThixo ekubuyeni kwaKhe.

UYesu usilungiselele indawo (Yohane 14:1–3), indawo entle emangalisa ngeyona ndlela ukuba ungayichaza. Eneneni, “ulwimi lobuntu alanelanga ukuchaza umvuzo wamalungisa. Uyakwaziwa kuphela ngabo bawubonayo. Akukho ngqondo iphelelwayo inokuqonda ubuqaqawuli beParadesi kaThixo.”—Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, p. 675.

Nangona singenako ngokwenene ukuqonda ukuba izulu elitsha nomhlaba omtsha ziyakuba njani, uThixo ubonisa uYohane umbono wale ndawo ukuze sikhangele phambili “kumsitho” ozakwenzeka kungekudala. Eneneni, simenyelwa ekuthini “sinyamekele izinto zaphezulu, zingabi zezasemhlabeni” (Kol. 3:2).

UThixo ngobunono ulungiselela esi siganeko, kwaye Yena akafuni ukuba lo “msitho” usiqubule (bona kuMat. 22:1–14, Mat. 25:1–13).

Elimiweyo lilibandla elizakusibona esi siganeko sisenzeka, kwaye singabanye beziqu ezisemqoka kweli bali. Sizakuthelela “kumtshakazi,” kulo mzi uYesu azakusisa kuwo ekuzeni kwaKhe kwesibini. Ngokusemdleni, abantu bakaThixo (abangcwele) bakwabizwa njengomtshakazi (bona Isityhi. 19:7), mhlawumbi ngenxa yokuba “bakumzi ongcwele, iYerusalem eNtsha, usihla uvela kuThixo, uphuma emazulwini, ulungisiwe njengomtshakazi ehonjiselwe indoda yakhe” (Isityhi. 21:2).

Le nkcazelo intle yoMzi oNgcwele ibonisa ukuba kukho uqhagamshelwano olusondeleyo phakathi kwabantu bakaThixo kunye nesixeko, kuba bobabini babizwa “umtshakazi.” IBhayibhile ityhila inkcazo enengcombolo “ngoMzi oNgcwele, iYerusalem eNtsha, elikomkhulu nommeli wobukumkani, [kwaye] ibizwa ngokuba ‘ngumtshakazi, umfazi weMvana.’ ”—Ellen G. White, The Great Controversy, p. 426.
