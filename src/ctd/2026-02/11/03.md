---
title:  Damta in
date:   08/06/2026
---

Galilee bualtui gei a mihonpi mitkha muh in. Zingsang tuung panin Zeisu hong tunkik ding a ngakuh hi a; Zeisu gunkuang sungpan hong paikhiat phetleh, kiim uum luungluung uh in, Capernaum khua a paina dongah zui ngeingai uhhi. Biakinn ukpa Jairus hong tungvat in a tanu damask dingin Zeisu hong thuum hi.

Mihon lakah numei khatpen, kum tampi cidamlo sa ahi hi. A sumneih sungteng zong siavuan tetung ah beimang khinzo hinapi “noptuam zawlo in gim semsem zaw hi” (Maku 5: 26). Hih Galilee mi lianpa’ thu tuanu in zakhin zo a, lam-etna nei ahih manin, zingsang tuung panin thasaan in innpan paikhia in mipi lakah hong kihel hamtang hi. Mihonpi in huu-bing zo liangzah dingin kiphukeei ahih manin letmat khatkhat ta in Zeisu’ kiang vanaih theibek hi. Tua bang a phutphut leh Zeisu a vamu theita hi. Amah ki thahatsak in “A puanmong nangawn valawngkha leng dam ning” (Maku 5: 28) cihi.

`Maku 5:21–34. Bangthu piang a, bang thusinna om hiam?`

Hih thupiang in, damlo leh pampaih mite leh mihonpi laka mangthangte Zeisu’ hong kepna leh hong khasiatna hong lak hi. Tuani in mi tampi in Zeisu’ gei ah hong kikai khawm ngeingai uh hinapi; mikhat bek in ama’ kisapphat mahmah thuphate sangdingin Zeisu’ kiang hong tungthei hi. Tuanu’ lawnna in amah damsak hizawlo a, ama’ upna in amah damask hizaw hi (Maku 5: 34).

“Honpa in upna tawh lawnna leh gin neilopi a mihonpi’ lawnna khentel thei hi”—Ellen G. White, The Desire of Ages, p. 344. Zeisu’ puan in bangmah vangtuam nei tuanlo hi. Numeinu in upna tawh baan in valawn ding a teelna in amah damask hi.

Tua a thanem numei in, haksat thuakna sungah innsung ah omlel peuhlo in; a cidamna dingin lam-etna lianpi tawh Zeisu tawh kikhawlhna hangin damsakna lam-en in hong pai hi. Zeisu galmuh leltawh kicing zolo ahih manin; anaina mahah hong zuan hi.

Zeisu in tuni in hong sam hi, “vangik pua in gim mite aw, Keikiangah hongpai un. Tawldam kong pia ding hi. Keima’ hakkol kisin un. Keimah in kiniamkhiat in Ka lungnem hi. Kei kiangah hong kisin un. Na kha un tawldamna ngah ding hi” (Mate 11: 28, 29).

`Hih numei in Rom. 5: 3-5 sunga ngaihdan te akisapna koibang in kilangsak hiam? Nangma nunna sungah koibang in a kimu tam maw?`
